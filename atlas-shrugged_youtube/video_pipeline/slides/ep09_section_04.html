Two Views of Desire
Lillian's Influence
Lillian has made Rearden feel that his desires are degrading. To her, sex is something base that a wife tolerates as a duty—a necessary evil of marriage. She performs it with cold resignation.
Lillian's View
Physical desire is an imposition, passion a flaw. To desire someone physically is to degrade them.
Dagny's View
Physical attraction is the expression of spiritual admiration—not its opposite. Desire is an act of celebration.
Dagny does not separate body from mind, desire from value. She desires Rearden because she admires him.
