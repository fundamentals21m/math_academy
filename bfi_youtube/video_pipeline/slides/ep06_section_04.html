The Rise and Fall of Cash
When Cash Lost Its Bearer Status
Pre-1971
Cash = Gold Claim
Banks secured gold. Cash was a bearer asset representing a claim on gold.
→
August 15, 1971
Nixon Shock
Gold window closed. Cash could no longer be exchanged for gold.
→
Post-1971
Fiat Currency
Cash lost its power as a bearer asset.
People didn't want to hold significant amounts out of fear their government would place further frictions on it.
