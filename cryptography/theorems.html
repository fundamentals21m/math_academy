Prime Numbers

Lesson 3
Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic
Every integer greater than 1 can be expressed as a unique product of prime numbers (up to reordering).
Lesson 3
Infinitude of Primes
There are infinitely many prime numbers. (Euclid, c. 300 BCE)

GCD & Euclidean Algorithm

Lesson 4
Bézout's Identity
For any integers a and b (not both zero), there exist integers x and y such that ax + by = GCD(a, b).

Modular Inverse

Lesson 5
Existence of Modular Inverse
The modular inverse of a (mod n) exists if and only if GCD(a, n) = 1.

Euler's Totient Function

Lesson 6
Euler's Theorem
If GCD(a, n) = 1, then aφ(n) ≡ 1 (mod n).

Cryptographic Applications

Lesson 9
RSA Correctness
For properly chosen e and d, decryption correctly recovers the original message: med ≡ m (mod n).
Lesson 6
Fermat's Little Theorem
If p is prime and GCD(a, p) = 1, then ap-1 ≡ 1 (mod p). A special case of Euler's Theorem.
