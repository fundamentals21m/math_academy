Number Systems: Binary, Decimal & Hex

Before diving into cryptography, you need to speak the language of computers. Computers think in binary (1s and 0s), but humans prefer decimal (0-9). Hexadecimal (0-F) is a convenient middle ground used everywhere in cryptography.

Why This Matters: Cryptographic keys, hashes, and addresses are almost always displayed in hexadecimal. Understanding these number systems is essential.

The Three Number Systems

Binary (Base 2)

Only two digits: 0 and 1

Each position is a power of 2

1010 = 8+0+2+0 = 10

Decimal (Base 10)

Ten digits: 0-9

Each position is a power of 10

255 = 200+50+5

Hexadecimal (Base 16)

Sixteen digits: 0-9, A-F

Each position is a power of 16

FF = 15×16 + 15 = 255

Understanding Place Values

In any number system, each position has a value based on the base raised to a power:

Decimal: 425

4 × 10² = 4 × 100 = 400
2 × 10¹ = 2 × 10 = 20
5 × 10⁰ = 5 × 1 = 5
Total: 400 + 20 + 5 = 425

Binary: 1101

1 × 2³ = 1 × 8 = 8
1 × 2² = 1 × 4 = 4
0 × 2¹ = 0 × 2 = 0
1 × 2⁰ = 1 × 1 = 1
Total: 8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 13 (decimal)

Hexadecimal Digits

Since hex needs 16 digits but we only have 0-9, we use letters A-F for values 10-15:

Hex 01234567 89ABCDEF
Dec 01234567 89101112131415
Bin 00000001001000110100010101100111 10001001101010111100110111101111
Key Insight: Each hex digit represents exactly 4 binary digits. This makes converting between binary and hex very easy!

Converting Decimal to Binary

To convert decimal to binary, repeatedly divide by 2 and track the remainders:

Example: Convert 13 to Binary

13 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 1
6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0
3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1
1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1
Read remainders bottom-up: 1101

Converting Decimal to Hex

Same process, but divide by 16:

Example: Convert 255 to Hex

255 ÷ 16 = 15 remainder 15 (F)
15 ÷ 16 = 0 remainder 15 (F)
Read remainders bottom-up: FF

Binary ↔ Hex Shortcut

Since 16 = 2⁴, each hex digit maps to exactly 4 binary digits. This makes conversion easy:

Example: Convert A7 (hex) to Binary

A = 10 = 1010
7 = 7 = 0111
Result: 10100111

Why Hex is Used in Cryptography

Compact

A 256-bit key is 64 hex characters vs 256 binary digits

Human Readable

Easier to read, compare, and type than long binary strings

Byte Aligned

2 hex digits = 1 byte = 8 bits. Perfect for computer memory!

Real Example: A Bitcoin address like 1A1zP1eP5QGefi2DMPTfTL5SLmv7DivfNa uses Base58 (similar to hex but with more characters) to represent a 160-bit hash.
